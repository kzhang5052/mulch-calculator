How much gravel for a fire pit or paver base?

Volume = area × depth ÷ 27 for cubic yards. For a round fire pit, area = π × (diameter ÷ 2)². A compacted base wants about 4 inches for patios and paths, 6+ inches under a driveway. Always compact in layers so it doesn't settle later.

How deep should a fire pit gravel base be?

3–4 inches of compacted gravel under the pit gives drainage and a stable, level base.

Gravel vs paver base — what's the difference?

Paver base is angular crushed stone with fines that lock together when compacted, ideal under pavers. Loose pea gravel is decorative and doesn't compact.

How much does it weigh?

Gravel runs ~2,700 lb per cubic yard, so order delivery or check your vehicle payload for anything over a half yard.
