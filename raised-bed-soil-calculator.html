How much soil for a raised bed?

Multiply length × width × fill depth (all in feet), then divide by 27 for cubic yards. A 4×8 bed at 12 inches deep is 32 cubic feet ≈ 1.2 yards. Buying that in bags gets expensive fast, so two or more beds usually justify bulk delivery.

What's a good raised bed soil mix?

Roughly 60% topsoil, 30% compost, 10% aeration (perlite or coarse sand). Compost feeds plants; topsoil gives structure.

How deep should the bed be?

10–12 inches suits most vegetables. Root crops like carrots prefer ~18 inches.

Do I need to fill it all with soil?

For deep beds, some gardeners fill the bottom third with logs/branches (hügelkultur) or coarse mulch to save on soil.
